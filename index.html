10 Billion Years

the life of a star — and how its death became you

chapter 01 · the cloud

It begins in darkness.

A cloud of hydrogen and dust, older than memory, drifting between the stars. Cold. Silent. Waiting. Move your hand through it — it remembers being touched.

chapter 02 · the collapse

Then — gravity whispers.

A shockwave passes. The cloud folds inward, spinning faster as it falls, a knot tightening for a hundred thousand years.

chapter 03 · a galaxy

Pull back.

This is happening everywhere. A hundred billion suns wheel through the dark — and in one quiet spiral arm, a small cloud is about to catch fire.

chapter 04 · first light

Pressure becomes fire.

At fifteen million degrees, hydrogen fuses into helium. Light floods outward for the first time. A star is born.

chapter 05 · the swelling

It burns for nine billion years.

Then the balance breaks. The core sinks, the skin swells — a red giant, vast enough to swallow worlds.

chapter 06 · the last fire

The fuel runs out.

Deep inside, the star forges its final gifts — carbon, oxygen, iron — and the iron heart grows too heavy to hold.

chapter 07 · supernova

One second.

The core collapses at a quarter of the speed of light and rebounds — a detonation that outshines the entire galaxy.

chapter 08 · afterwards

The debris drifts for eons.

Carbon for your cells. Iron for your blood. Calcium for your bones. The dust gathers, cools, and one day opens its eyes.

You are made of stars.

Every atom heavier than helium in your body was forged in one.
